JUDICIAL Act of 2024

Summary

The "Justice for University Decisions, Integrity, and Academic Litigation" Act, also known as the JUDICIAL Act of 2024, transfers the authority to adjudicate academic hearing board cases to the UH SGA Department of Justice.

Details

Short Title

This Act may be cited as the "JUDICIAL Act of 2024".

Findings

  • The SGA of the University of Houston is vested with the authority to represent the interests of the student body.
  • The current process for adjudicating academic hearing board cases is administered by university officials, which can lead to delays and concerns regarding impartiality and fairness.
  • Transferring the adjudicative authority to the SGA DOJ will enhance transparency, accountability, and responsiveness to the concerns of the student body.
  • The SGA DOJ, composed of student representatives, is uniquely positioned to understand and address the needs and rights of the student body.
  • Establishing a clear and structured framework within the SGA DOJ will ensure an equitable and efficient process for handling academic hearing board cases.

Definitions

  • Academic Hearing Board: The body responsible for hearing and adjudicating cases related to academic misconduct, including but not limited to plagiarism, cheating, and other violations of academic integrity.
  • Department of Justice (DOJ): The judicial branch of the UH SGA, responsible for upholding and enforcing the SGA Constitution and Bylaws.

Transfer of Authority

The authority to adjudicate academic hearing board cases shall be transferred from the university administration to the UH SGA Department of Justice.

  • Effective Date: This transfer shall take effect immediately upon the passage of this Act and approval by the University President.
  • Responsibilities of the SGA DOJ: The Chief Justice of the SGA DOJ shall oversee the transfer and management of academic hearing board cases. A dedicated Academic Hearing Board within the SGA DOJ shall be appointed, consisting of trained student justices.

Structure and Roles within the SGA DOJ

  • Chief Justice: Oversees all judicial activities and ensures compliance with university policies.
  • Chair of the Academic Hearing Board: Responsible for leading hearings and ensuring procedural fairness.
  • Student Justices: Responsible for hearing cases, reviewing evidence, and rendering decisions.
  • Clerk of the Academic Hearing Board: Maintains records, schedules hearings, and ensures communication between parties.

Legality and Compliance

This transfer of authority is in accordance with the UH SGA Constitution and Bylaws, which empower the SGA to manage student affairs and uphold academic integrity.

Implementation and Oversight

An oversight committee shall be established to monitor the implementation of this Act, comprising representatives from the SGA DOJ, university administration, and student organizations. The committee shall convene on a monthly basis to review progress, address any issues, and ensure compliance with the guidelines and procedures established in this Act.

TRANSFER Act of 2024

Summary

The "Transferring Authority, Navigating Student Finances, Events, and Resources" Act, also known as the TRANSFER Act of 2024, transfers the authority to fund student organizations, oversee major campus events, and manage investments to the UH SGA.

Details

Short Title

This Act may be cited as the "TRANSFER Act of 2024".

Findings

  • The SGA of the University of Houston represents the official voice of the student body.
  • Funding for student organizations is critical to their operations and the overall student experience.
  • The current process for funding student organizations is managed by university administration, which can lead to delays and misalignment with student needs.
  • Transferring the authority to fund student organizations to the SGA will enhance transparency, accountability, and responsiveness to student needs.
  • The SGA, with its elected student representatives, is better positioned to understand and address the financial needs of student organizations.
  • Major campus events, such as Homecoming and Frontier Fiesta, play a significant role in student life and should be managed by a body that directly represents student interests.
  • Authorizing the SGA to invest in bonds and other financial instruments will provide additional financial stability and growth opportunities for student organization funding.
  • The Student Fee Advisory Committee (SFAC) oversees the allocation of student service fees to support various non-academic services, programs, and activities that enhance student life on campus.
  • Transferring the responsibilities of the SFAC to the SGA will consolidate student governance and financial oversight under a single representative body, improving efficiency and alignment with student interests.

Definitions

  • Student Organization: Any registered student group at the University of Houston.
  • Funding: Financial support provided for the activities, events, and operational costs of student organizations.
  • Major Campus Events: Large-scale events such as Homecoming, Frontier Fiesta, and similar university-wide events.
  • Investments: Financial investments in bonds, stocks, or other financial instruments authorized by the university’s investment policies.
  • Student Fee Advisory Committee (SFAC): The committee that oversees the allocation of student service fees.

Transfer of Funding Authority

The authority to allocate funds to student organizations shall be transferred from the university administration to the UH SGA.

  • Effective Date: This transfer shall take effect immediately upon the passage of this Act and approval by the University President.
  • Responsibilities of the SGA: The Vice President of the SGA shall oversee the transfer and management of student organization funding. A transparent and fair process for reviewing and approving funding requests from student organizations shall be established.

Transfer of Control Over Major Campus Events

The authority to plan, manage, and oversee major campus events shall be transferred from the university administration to the UH SGA.

  • Effective Date: This transfer shall take effect immediately upon the passage of this Act and approval by the University President.
  • Responsibilities of the SGA: The Director of Campus Events, a newly established position within the SGA, shall oversee the planning and execution of major campus events. Committees dedicated to the planning and execution of major campus events shall be established.

Authorization to Invest in Bonds and Other Financial Instruments

The UH SGA is hereby authorized to invest in bonds, stocks, and other financial instruments in accordance with the university’s investment policies.

  • Effective Date: This authorization shall take effect immediately upon the passage of this Act and approval by the University President.
  • Responsibilities of the SGA: The Treasurer of the SGA shall oversee the management and reporting of investments. An Investment Committee, consisting of SGA representatives and financial advisors, shall be established to guide investment decisions.

Transfer of Student Fee Advisory Committee (SFAC) Responsibilities

The responsibilities of the Student Fee Advisory Committee (SFAC) shall be transferred to the UH SGA.

  • Effective Date: This transfer shall take effect immediately upon the passage of this Act and approval by the University President.
  • Responsibilities of the SGA: The SGA President shall oversee the integration of SFAC responsibilities within the SGA structure. A subcommittee within the SGA shall be established to manage the allocation of student service fees, ensuring transparency, accountability, and alignment with student needs.

Implementation and Oversight

An oversight committee shall be established to monitor the implementation of this Act, comprising representatives from the SGA, university administration, and student organizations. The committee shall convene on a monthly basis to review progress, address any issues, and ensure compliance with the guidelines and procedures established in this Act.
